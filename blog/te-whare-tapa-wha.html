What is Te Whare Tapa Whā?

Te whare tapa whā is a model of the 4 dimensions of wellbeing developed by Sir Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health. The 4 dimensions are: taha tinana (physical wellbeing) taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing) taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing) taha whānau (family wellbeing). With 4 walls, the wharenui (meeting house) is a symbol of these 4 dimensions. The wharenui’s connection with the whenua (land) forms the foundation for the other 4 dimensions.

Actions
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)
  • Getting out of the house everyday for fresh air
  • Go for walks regularly
  • Get enough sleep
  • Make sure to stay hydrated
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)
  • Spending quality time with family at least once a week
  • Have meals with family regularly
  • Make sure to help family out
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)
  • Getting out of the house everyday for fresh air
  • Stay social
  • Practice Mindfulness
  • Talk about things
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)
  • Practice Mindfulness
  • Connect with things more
  • Be more aware if things
  • Have quiet time
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)
  • Do some gardening or walk outside at least once a week
  • Getting out of the house everyday for fresh air
