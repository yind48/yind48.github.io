Identities and Values

An exploration of person and values

Situation where ethical decision making has been required

Prior to my current life I was a Immigration Officer for Immigration NZ. Where I had to make numerous decisions that presented multiple ethical conundrum's. However, I am proud to say I have played my part in keeping dream's alive, families together and creating many happy moments in people's live's. I have also unfortunately played my part in the other scenario's too. I reflect on these decision's knowing that I did the best I could both professionally and as a fellow human.

How culture and people have influenced my values and identity

I believe peoples culture and company have a significant influence on the values and and identities we adopt and present. I personally have felt the influence in my life when reflecting on my own identity and values. I believe the nuture side of my upbringing from my family to my friends ultimately drove my actions and intentions which has led to who I call 'me' today.

Identifying strengths and their effects on my learning

My strengths that have stood out and I am proud to say I am hardworking and always willing to trying my best. This has been reflected in all that I do, professionally and personally. I believe this will ensure my work ethics in up to standard as the journey continues.

Identifying limitations and their effects on my learning

I am of the understanding no one is perfect and no one can be perfect but we are simply perfectly imperfect. Sometimes when I come across paths where I feel imperfect this leads to me feeling a little demotivated. I do understand my limitations and weaknesses, however, can only hope I remain proactive and motivated to navigate the difficulties in my learning and career.

Reflection on a time where I have worked collaboratively and how I navigated any difficulties

Professionally and academically collaboration has been a major component. Many a times idea's are recieved as you would hope. I just reflect and try to understand that we are all different and think in our own way. The simplest way is to try to understand each others thought process for that decision and being open minded. This leads to a unobstructed path to compromise where the best of both idea's come together.
