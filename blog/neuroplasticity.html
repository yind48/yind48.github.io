Neuroplasticity

What is Neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity is the brain's ability to change and adapt due to experience. This happens through the changing, reorganisation and growth of neural networks that occur due to learning and exposure.

This benefits your:

  • Ability to learn new things
  • Enhances you cognitive capabilities
  • Recover or repair functionality, eg from brain injuries or stroke
  • Strength brain function and knowledge
  • Reduce the decline of brain function

How to use it with my learning?

By understanding the hypothesis of neuroplasticity I am able to incorporate it into my learning and improve my understanding of how the learning process works.

How to improve your neuroplasticity?

Generate an environment with lots of stimulus that allows focus, attention and challenges.

    Such as:

  • Learning a new language
  • Learning a new topic
  • Learning an instrument
  • Travelling exploring
  • Reading

Growth Mindset

What is a Growth Mindset?

Growth mindset is the belief system that an individual's own talents can be developed and improved through hard-work, strategies and support. It’s relevance stems from its benefits as a positive thinking mechanism that allows a student to understand they are not limited.
